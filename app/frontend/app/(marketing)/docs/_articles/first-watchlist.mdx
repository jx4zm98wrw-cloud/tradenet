<h2 className="docs-h2">
  <span className="docs-h2-num">01</span> Pick a brand to watch
</h2>

<p className="docs-p">
  Start with a brand you know well — usually one of your client's registered marks. The clearer you are about what conflicts <em>look like</em>, the easier it is to dial in the watchlist's sensitivity.
</p>

<h2 className="docs-h2">
  <span className="docs-h2-num">02</span> Decide your match criteria
</h2>

<p className="docs-p">
  A watchlist combines four kinds of criteria: name (text or phonetic), image (upload a specimen), classes (Nice classification), and applicant filters. Most clearance watchlists use all four.
</p>

<DocsCallout heading="Recommendation">
  Start broad and tighten. A watchlist that returns 12 false positives the first week is better than one that misses the real conflict on week three.
</DocsCallout>

<h2 className="docs-h2">
  <span className="docs-h2-num">03</span> Tune the similarity threshold
</h2>

<p className="docs-p">
  The threshold slider controls how aggressive the matcher is. For pharmaceutical brands (Class 5) we recommend starting at <strong>0.75</strong>; for retail (Class 35) you can go as low as <strong>0.60</strong> without drowning in noise.
</p>

<h2 className="docs-h2">
  <span className="docs-h2-num">04</span> Set up alerts
</h2>

<p className="docs-p">
  Email digests are on by default. On Firm and Enterprise plans, connect Slack or Microsoft Teams so the whole matter team sees findings in the same place you're already working.
</p>
