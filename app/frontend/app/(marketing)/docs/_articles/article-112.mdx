<h2 className="docs-h2">
  <span className="docs-h2-num">01</span> The 5-month rule
</h2>

<p className="docs-p">
  Under Article 112 of the IP Law (as amended 2019, 2022), any third party may file opposition to a published application within <strong>five months from the publication date</strong>. The clock starts on the date the application appears in the gazette, not the application date.
</p>

<DocsCallout heading="Important">
  The 5-month window is calendar months, not 150 days. February publications get a slightly shorter window than July ones.
</DocsCallout>

<h2 className="docs-h2">
  <span className="docs-h2-num">02</span> What you can oppose on
</h2>

<ul className="docs-list">
  <li>Identity or confusing similarity with an earlier registered or applied-for mark</li>
  <li>Bad faith (especially common with well-known foreign marks)</li>
  <li>Descriptive or generic content</li>
  <li>Conflict with a geographical indication</li>
  <li>Misleading character (origin, quality, nature of goods)</li>
</ul>

<h2 className="docs-h2">
  <span className="docs-h2-num">03</span> Filing logistics
</h2>

<p className="docs-p">
  Oppositions are filed with Vietnam IP, usually through an authorized IP agent. Filing fees are modest (VND 550,000–2,200,000 depending on classes), but the substantive workload — gathering prior-rights evidence, drafting the opposition brief — is where the cost lives.
</p>

<DocsCallout heading="After the window closes">
  If you miss the 5-month opposition window, your only recourse is invalidation proceedings — slower (typically 18–36 months), more expensive, and with a higher evidentiary bar. The opposition window is genuinely your best shot.
</DocsCallout>
